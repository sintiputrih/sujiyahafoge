Ross Butler Early Life

Actor Ross Fleming Butler was born in Singapore on May 17, 1990, and subsequently obtained US citizenship. His roles as Brett Willis on the Disney Channel series “K.C. Despite being originally cast as Reggie Mantle in the popular Netflix/CW series “Riverdale,” Butler decided to leave the role after just one season since he had already committed to filming “13 Reasons Why.”

Butler made a significant career move into the superhero genre in 2019 when he played Eugene Choi in the highly anticipated movie “Shazam!” Butler also entered the DC Extended Universe. He returned to the part in the 2023 sequel “Shazam! Fury of the Gods” after receiving praise for his performance. Butler achieved a noteworthy accomplishment with this, demonstrating his adaptability as an actor in both the television and film industries.

The birthplace of Ross Fleming Butler is Singapore, where he was born to a father of English and Dutch ancestry and a mother of Chinese and Malaysian ethnicity. Before moving to the United States at the age of four, he spent his formative years in Jakarta, Indonesia. Butler was raised by his mother in Fairfax, Virginia, a suburban community outside of Washington, D.C., and his early years were influenced by the diversity of cultures in his family.
