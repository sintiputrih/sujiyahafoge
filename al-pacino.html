Professional Life

Alfredo dropped out of school at the age of 17 after failing practically all of his classes except English. To fund his acting lessons, he worked as a messenger, busboy, janitor, and postal clerk. During this period, he also appeared in basement plays in New York’s theatrical underworld but was turned down for the Actors Studio. He later became a member of the ‘Herbert Bergh of Studio’ (HB Studio).

Pacino began appearing in theatre shows shortly after, and in 1969 he got a Tony Award for his performance in the Broadway play ‘Does a Tiger Wear a Necktie?’ In the 1971 film ‘The Panic in Needle Park,’ he played ‘Bobby.’ Although the film was not a commercial success, his acting abilities drew recognition. With the publication of ‘The Godfather’ in 1972, Alfredo achieved incredible celebrity. The film gained widespread critical acclaim and was nominated for three Academy Awards.

Alfredo received the Palme d’Or at the Cannes Film Festival in 1973 for his performance as Francis Lionel ‘Lion’ Delbuchi in ‘Scarecrow.’ In the same year, he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actor for his performance in ‘Serpico,’ a film based on the actual life of New York City cop Frank Serpico.

He appeared in numerous hit films during the 1990s, including Frankie and Johnny in 1991, Glengarry Glen Ross in 1992, Scent of a Woman in 1992, Heat in 1995, Donnie Brasco in 1997, The Devil’s Advocate in 1997, and others. In 2002, he appeared in the film Insomnia which was about the strange murder of a young lady. In 2007, he also appeared in the mega film ‘Ocean’s Thirteen.’
