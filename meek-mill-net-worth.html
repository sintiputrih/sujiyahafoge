Meek Mill: Music, Activism, Dream Chasers Records, Endorsement

Music

Meek Mill’s primary source of wealth was his desire to become a successful rapper. Young Jeezy, Clipse, Trick Daddy, Jay Z, Juelz Santana, and Lil Wayne are just a few rappers who have impacted him.

Mill resumed pursuing a solo music career after The Bloodhoundz split up, using the same identity as before.

The mixtapes “The Real Me” and “The Real Me 2,” both released in late 2006, were his debut studio projects.

Meek Mill’s fortune arose due to the rapper’s release of a few more mixtapes, which led to him being given many recording deals.

He released four mixtapes between 2008 and 2010, titled “Flamers,” “Flamers 2,” “Flamers 2,5”, and “Flamers 3,” all of which were commercially successful.

After the release of his first mixtape in 2009, he grabbed the notice of T.I., a well-known artist and producer who decided to sign him to his Grand Hustle label.

The rapper’s contract with Grand Hustle provided him with a plethora of chances, and he became not only wealthy but also famous as a result of it.

Activism

After his release from state custody in 2018, Mill instantly became a leading champion for criminal justice reform in the United States. He co-founded REFORM Alliance with fellow musician and entrepreneur Jay-Z.

The charitable organization company’s goal is to “dramatically reduce the number of people who are unfairly under the influence of the criminal justice system – starting with parole and probation.”

It intends to accomplish this by bringing together leaders from various fields, including business, entertainment, government, sports, technology, and more, who share a common interest in contributing and advocating for those who are unjustly under the influence of criminal justice system in the US.

Dream Chasers Records

Meek Mill announced his record label, Dream Chasers Records, on October 26, 2012, with the signature artists Louie V. Gutta, Lee Mazin, and Goldie.

Meek Mill officially announced the debut of Dream Chasers Records, a partnership with Roc Nation, on July 24, 2019.

Mill commented on the agreement, saying, “Creating a record company has always been a goal of mine as a businessman, and I am grateful for Roc Nation and Jay-support Z’s in this endeavor.

I want to leverage my music industry experience to identify new, eager talent and open opportunities for the future generation of musicians.”

The label will also be in charge of its operations, creative strategy, marketing, and financial matters.

Jay-Z commented on the collaboration, saying: “Everything he’s done so far demonstrates that he’s ready to [lead] the next generation. We take a step back and look at the big picture; this is about more than just signing musicians and having hit records.”

Mill will lead a team in the label’s corporate New York office and assist with operating a recording facility for the label’s artists as president.
