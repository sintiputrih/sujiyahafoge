Early Life and Biography

Sharon Osbourne was born Sharon Rachel Levy on October 9, 1952, in Brixton, London, England, to Don Arden and Hope Shaw. Her father was a well-known entrepreneur and worked in the music industry. On the other side, her mother was a ballet dancer. She has a brother, David Arden, who is her only sibling. Sharon is of Ashkenazi Jewish and Irish ancestry. Her childhood was not idyllic, and she was surrounded by violence as a result of her father’s actions.

Personal Life: Boyfriends, Husbands, and Children

Sharon married Ozzy Osbourne, a well-known English singer, on July 4, 1982 in Hawaii, and the two have been together ever since. Kelly and Aimee Osbourne are their two daughters, while Jack Osbourne is their son. When she was 18 years old and working for her father, she met Ozzy for the first time.

However, just because they’ve been married for so long doesn’t mean they’ve had an easy time. The pair used to fight frequently, and in August 1989, Ozzy was arrested for attempted murder after attempting to choke her. As a result, Ozzy was forced to spend six months in treatment.

A Professional Life

Sharon Osbourne got her start in the business by working for her father. When she started managing her future husband, Ozzy Osbourne, it was a watershed moment in her life. Encouraged by her success as his manager, Sharon Osbourne created Sharon Osbourne Management, a talent management firm. Gary Moore, Motörhead, Lita, and Ford have all been managed by her company.

When it came to her TV career, she shot to stardom after starring in the wildly famous TV series “The Osbournes,” which was based on Ozzy’s family’s lives. The series lasted four seasons and 52 episodes, and it aired from 2002 to 2005. In 2003, she debuted her TV show, “The Sharon Osbourne Show,” however it was canceled after only one season due to low ratings.

From 2004 until 2007, she was a judge on the popular UK reality TV show “The X Factor.” She returned to the show as a judge in 2013 and again from 2016 to 2017. In 2006, she returned to the aforementioned show’s spin-off show, “The X Factor: Battle of the Stars,” as a judge. The following year, she was cast as a judge on the second season of the popular talent show “America’s Got Talent” (2007).
