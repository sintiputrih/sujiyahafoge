Zach Eflin | Professional Baseball Career

Zach Eflin started his professional Baseball career after getting selected by the San Diego Padres in the first round (33rd overall) at the 2012 MLB draft.

After getting drafted, Eflin signed with the AZL Padres in the Arizona League and started playing for them.

He spent two seasons playing with the Padres and was traded to the Philadelphia Phillies for exchange and cash in 2014. With Philips, also Eflin played incredibly and continuously showed improvements.

For his outstanding performance, he was also rated as the fifth-best prospect in the Phillies’ system by Baseball America in 2015.  Likewise, that season he also got named to the Eastern League Pitcher of the week and Eastern League All-Star.

Later, for the 2016 season, Eflin received an invitation to spring training and started the season with Lehigh Valley IronPigs. That season Zach played excellently and was named the League’s Pitcher of the week.

Moreover, on June 14, 2016, he made his MLB debut against the Toronto Blue Jays. But the debut did not go well; in fact, his debut was known as the worst debut in the Phillies and baseball history.

Zach Eflin’s Velocity and split

Eflin’s average fastball velocity went from 93.8 mph in 2020 to 92.3 mph in 2021. There is a difference of 1.5 mph in speed. However, he is still up from other dimensions.

His hard-hit rate elevated from 32.7 percent in 2020 to 48.8 percent in 2021. It is the seventh-highest overall hard-hit rate among pitchers qualified for this season.

Eflin has thrown 8,458 pitches between 2016 and 2021 in the MLB Regular Season. He has relied primarily on his Sinker (93mph) and Slider (88mph) in 2021. He has also mixed in a Fourseam Fastball (93mph), curve (78mph), and change (85mph).
