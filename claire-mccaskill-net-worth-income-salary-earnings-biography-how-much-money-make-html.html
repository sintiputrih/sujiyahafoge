Claire Conner McCaskill (/m??ksk?l/; born July 24, 1953) is a United States Senator from Missouri and a member of the Democratic Party. She was the first woman elected to the United States Senate from Missouri in her own right, defeating Republican incumbent Jim Talent by 49.6 percent to 47.3 percent in the 2006 election. When Kit Bond retired in 2011, she became the state’s senior U.S. Senator, and she won re-election in 2012, beating Republican Todd Akin by a margin of 54.7 percent to 39.2 percent.  McCaskill was the State Auditor of Missouri from 1999 to 2007, before being elected to the United States Senate. From 1993 to 1998, she was the prosecutor for Jackson County, and from 1983 to 1989, she was a member of the Missouri House of Representatives. In the 2004 election, she ran for Governor of Missouri, defeating Democratic incumbent Bob Holden in the primary but losing in a tight general election to Republican Matt Blunt. She is a Rolla native who attended the University of Missouri and Georgetown University. McCaskill is a member of the Armed Services Committee, the Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation, the Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs, and the Special Committee on Aging in the United States Senate. She is the head of Homeland Security’s Contracting Oversight Subcommittee.
