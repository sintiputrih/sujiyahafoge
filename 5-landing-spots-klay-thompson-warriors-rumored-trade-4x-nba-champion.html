5 landing spots for Klay Thompson after Warriors are rumored to trade 4x NBA champion

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, May 21, 2024

Golden State Warriors star Klay Thompson hasn't been impressive so far this season. He's struggled to make major contributions to the team, prompting speculation about his future in San Francisco.

The 6-foot-6 shooting guard's struggles have contributed to Golden State's 6-7 start this season, with the team losing its last five games.

Thompson is averaging 13.8 points, 3.4 rebounds, and 1.9 assists for Golden State this season. The five-time All-Star is shooting 40.1% from the field and 32.9% from downtown. These are his second-lowest averages for his career, causing problems for the team offensively.

The Warriors are in a bit of a tough situation without Steph Curry due to an injury and Draymond Green due to a five-game suspension, meaning Thompson's struggles have come at the worst possible time.

As such, rumors have started swirling on social media about the Warriors potentially trading Thompson soon.

Here are five potential landing spots for the Warriors star if he is traded.

#1. Philadelphia 76ers

Tobias Harris has an expiring contract this season and the Philadelphia 76ers could save themselves some trouble if he gets traded soon. One player that the Sixers could look at is Thompson, who's experienced enough to help the team with their goal of winning a title.

But with their recent success after trading James Harden, they might not be inclined to make another trade.

#2. Atlanta Hawks

The Atlanta Hawks lost a bit of an edge on the offensive side when they traded away Kevin Huerter. His replacements haven't lived up to expectations and Klay could be the one to help the team.

Atlanta already has a great backcourt, but it needs an elite shooter to complete the team.

#3. New York Knicks

Klay Thompson is still a capable shooter and he could fit well with the New York Knicks. The team struggles to fit RJ Barrett in at times and it could be a sign for them to move on from the Canadian wing.

Thompson's experience will also give the Knicks an edge in the East.

#4. Chicago Bulls

The Chicago Bulls have made Zach LaVine available and the Warriors may jump at the opportunity to sign him, sending Thompson the other way. LaVine is a great wing player who can create his own shots, which the team could benefit from if they choose to trade for him.

#5. Miami Heat

The Miami Heat missed out on Damian Lillard over the summer. While that hasn't affected them much this season, it will still be useful to trade for Klay Thompson and strengthen their core.

Adding another shooter will give the Heat dominance against the other teams in the East.
